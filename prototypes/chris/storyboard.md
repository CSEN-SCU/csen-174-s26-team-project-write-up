# Storyboard: Write Up (Ethan)

## Generated 6-Frame Storyline

**Frame 1: Introducing the Main Character**
- Ethan Park is a 20-year-old computer science and engineering student.
- He took AP Lang in high school and some college English classes, so he is a decent writer, but he still struggles with grammar, run-on sentences, and overusing simple words.
- He writes quickly and thinks fast, but his written ideas can come out jumbled and less coherent than he intends.

**Frame 2: The Problem Emerges**
- Ethan writes emails, class submissions, and technical communication in a conversational "thinking out loud" style.
- He often does not have time to deeply revise, so he uses AI or Grammarly to clean up coherence, tone, and repetitive mistakes.
- The result is functional writing, but he is not building durable skill because the same issues keep returning.

**Frame 3: The "Oh Crap" Moment**
- Ethan sends an urgent email to a professor/teammate, but the message is confusing and the tone lands wrong.
- He realizes that in high-stakes communication, he cannot reliably trust his first draft and keeps depending on external tools to avoid being misunderstood.

**Frame 4: The Solution Appears**
- Ethan first sees a YouTube ad for Write Up that feels practical and free, not another paid class or generic correction service.
- A classmate later recommends it too, saying they also found Grammarly less helpful for long-term improvement.
- Ethan tries Write Up because it promises to teach patterns instead of rewriting everything for him.

**Frame 5: The "Aha" Moment**
- Write Up detects Ethan's recurring mistakes across multiple writing samples and clearly highlights the repeated pattern.
- Instead of auto-fixing the text, it explains why the mistake happens and how to reframe his thinking so he can correct it himself.
- Even during urgent writing, he gets guidance on what to fix and why, without losing his own voice to a full rewrite.

**Frame 6: Life After the Solution**
- Ethan still writes quickly, but now he pauses earlier to structure ideas before typing, which improves clarity and concision.
- His confidence rises because he can produce clear, respectful, correctly toned communication without always needing AI to "rescue" the draft.
- He relies less on correction tools, and his writing becomes more coherent and intentional on the first pass.

## Visual Style
- Minimal, monochrome storyboard sketches.
- Simple scenes with clear expressions and context cues (laptop, email draft, class setting, quick feedback panel).
- Emphasize emotional shift: uncertainty -> urgency -> confidence.

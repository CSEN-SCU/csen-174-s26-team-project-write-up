Write Up

Feedback on your words—not a replacement essay.

Write Up is a Chrome extension for people who want clearer, more confident writing in school and at work. You choose what to work on (like vocabulary or tone), paste text you wrote, and get targeted suggestions and short explanations—plus a place to save phrases you want to reuse.

What Write Up is

A browser extension that acts as a lightweight writing coach: it works on text you supply, surfaces places to improve (especially vocabulary and tone), explains why a change might help, and can remember phrases you want to keep practicing. It is not meant to generate whole assignments or emails for you.

Who it’s for

Students (high school through college) and early-career professionals who write in English for classes, applications, lab reports, or workplace email—and want to sound clearer and more polished without losing their own voice. Also useful for anyone who feels stuck looking up synonyms mid-draft.

Good fit if: you already write your own drafts and want structured feedback and vocabulary you can revisit.
Not the goal if: you want one click to produce a finished essay or message with no authorial effort—use a different tool; Write Up is built for learning. Not a ghostwriter

What problem it solves

Many writers know their output “isn’t quite there” but don’t have a clear picture of recurring gaps—word choice, tone, or phrasing—across real work. Spell-checkers and grammar tools often fix the current line without building skill; full-draft AI can erase authenticity. Write Up focuses on your draft, your chosen goals, and repeatable improvement you can take to the next document.

How to use Write Up (in the app)

Once the extension is installed, someone with zero prior context can follow these steps inside Write Up:

01

Set your focus

When you start (or in settings), pick what you want to improve first—e.g. vocabulary, tone, or clarity—so suggestions match your goal.

02

Paste your own draft

Copy a paragraph or full message you wrote into Write Up. The extension responds to your text, not a blank “write me an essay” prompt.

03

Review and apply

Read highlighted spots, optional rephrasings, and short rationales. Accept or adapt changes in your real document (Google Doc, email, etc.).

04

Save vocabulary (optional)

Store words and phrases you want to reuse or drill later, so improvement carries across drafts.

With the Write Up extension installed, opening this section (URL ends with #try) asks Chrome to open the Write Up side panel automatically—use the nav link “Try it” or any “How to try it” button above.

  1. Start the API: python app.py in prototypes/ishika/server (defaults to http://127.0.0.1:5050).
  2. Load the extension: Chrome → chrome://extensions → turn on Developer mode → Load unpacked → choose prototypes/ishika/extension.
  3. Pin Write Up (puzzle icon → pin), then click the Write Up toolbar icon to open the side panel (like Grammarly’s docked panel).
  4. Paste text in the side panel, choose focus (vocabulary / tone / clarity), and click Get feedback.
  5. Optional: use the full web feedback page for a larger layout (button below).

Side panel needs Chrome 116+. If you change the server port, update host_permissions in extension/manifest.json and the saved API URL in the panel.

Product guardrails

Write Up is designed to teach and sharpen, not to homogenize voice: feedback should support clarity and professionalism while respecting how you sound—including dialect and personal style where appropriate.

# Storyboard: Lydia's Writing Growth Journey

## Generated 6-Frame Storyline

### Frame 1: Introducing the Main Character
Lydia is a college student in her early twenties preparing applications for writing-heavy engineering internships. She is already a strong writer, but wants to reach a more polished, professional level for high-stakes opportunities.

### Frame 2: The Problem Emerges
She needs to submit strong writing samples and also perform well in live, timed writing evaluations. Her challenge is not basic writing ability; it is knowing which advanced skills to sharpen and how to close subtle gaps that could hurt her chances.

### Frame 3: The "Oh Crap" Moment
Right before an internship interview she really wants, Lydia learns there will be a timed writing segment. She worries that recurring mistakes and uncertainty under pressure will show up at exactly the wrong moment and cost her the offer.

### Frame 4: The Solution Appears
Career advisors at her college recommend Write Up for focused, skill-building practice. Lydia is hesitant because she does not want to lose her own writing voice, but she tries it after seeing that the product emphasizes learning and style preservation rather than rewriting for her.

### Frame 5: The "Aha" Moment
Lydia uploads samples and completes guided activities. Write Up identifies recurring patterns, explains why each issue matters, offers multiple fix options, and gives targeted practice, especially around semicolon usage. During a timed practice write, she notices she is using semicolons correctly without second-guessing herself, and realizes she is improving while keeping her own style.

### Frame 6: Life After the Solution
Lydia enters the real assessment confident, performs strongly, and gets the internship. Long-term, she returns to Write Up whenever she feels rusty or needs to adapt to a new writing style, continuing to grow as a clear, versatile, self-directed writer.

## Visual Direction

- Minimal details focused on emotion and key actions, not polished UI

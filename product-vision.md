# Product Vision

## Modified Moore's Template (7 Fields)
**FOR** aspiring writers and students in the English language  
**WHO** need a clear roadmap of their linguistic blind spots, rather than just a quick fix for typos  
**THE** Write Up product is a personalized writing mentor and diagnostic dashboard  
**THAT** builds a "Linguistic Profile" of writing habits over time, identifying persistent patterns in syntax, tone, and vocabulary  
**UNLIKE** Grammarly, which acts as an automated editor focused on instant corrections  
**OUR PRODUCT** acts as a long-term tutor, providing pedagogical feedback, explaining why errors happen, and assigning targeted practice based on each user's recurring mistakes  
**POWERED BY** Recursive Linguistic Diagnostics (RLD) to track complex syntax and user-specific writing patterns, plus Generative Adaptive Learning to create practice activities in the user's own context and style

## How Might We (HMW)
How might we help writers see persistent patterns in their own work, get plain-language explanations, and complete targeted practice that stays close to their real contexts and voice - without the system rewriting for them or flattening dialect and style - as we aim to build durable writing skill for people who are often told they "already know English" and do not get sustained, fair support?

## Key Problem Framing Canvas Insights
- Writers often receive one-off correction instead of longitudinal feedback, which causes repeated mistakes and slow skill growth.
- Learning outcomes and voice preservation must be treated as core constraints, not optional enhancements.
- Multilingual learners, dyslexic writers, and users of non-dominant dialects are disproportionately affected by generic correction tools.
- Existing tools and incentives frequently optimize for speed and engagement over durable learning and fair support.
- The product should separate "teach me" from "fix it for me" and avoid AI behaviors that replace user thinking.

# Grammar and punctuation for coaching

Focus on high-impact issues that affect comprehension and credibility:

- Sentence boundaries: If two independent thoughts are joined with only a comma, suggest a period, semicolon, or a conjunction.
- Terminal punctuation: Complete sentences should usually end with a period, question mark, or exclamation mark.
- Repeated punctuation: Multiple exclamation points or question marks can be expressive, but frequent clustering can reduce perceived precision in formal contexts.
- Spacing consistency: Double spaces and inconsistent spacing are mechanical issues, not style choices.

## Teach with minimal edits

Prefer small examples over rewrites. If showing a correction, provide only one optional micro-edit and explain the reader-facing reason.

## Voice safeguard

Do not equate informality with incorrectness. Preserve contractions, rhythm, and spoken phrasing unless they directly obscure meaning.
